1. Bookmarklet

Why should I use the bookmarklet?

The bookmarklet operates from your browser toolbar to capture the web page displayed in your browser and analyses it for bibliographic information. It also provides a range of other functions, such as importing links from a web page or search list. It runs within your browser and is entirely benign - use with confidence.

How do I add the bookmarklet to my browser links?
  • Well behaved browser: drag the hyperlink [ # Heurist-Bookmarklet # ] to the Bookmarks or Links toolbar;
     
  • If this doesn't work: right click on the hyperlink and select Add to Favorites and select the Links toolbar.
    Note: You can rename the link after adding to the toolbar by right clicking and selecting Properties
 

2. Home page

Why should I make Heurist my home page?

Heurist is designed to load rapidly and provide you immediate access to your most commonly used web sites, references and notes (and other resources). It will find you less commonly used resources with a rapid Google-like search. It even incorporates a Google tab for searching the web. You will never need another home page or browser bookmarks!

How do I make Heurist my home page?

  • Preferred:   Drag this link [ HEURIST ] to the Home button of your browser.
     
  • Alternative: If the above does not work, open Tools > Options or Tools > Internet Options
    and insert the following as your home page address: http://HeuristScholar.org
     
  • If you aren't (yet) prepared to make Heurist your home page, drag the link to your Bookmarks or Links toolbar.

3. Word citation tool

What does it do?

Installing the citation places three buttons in a Word toolbar. Their functions are as follows:

  1. a reference search which finds references in the Heurist database and inserts them in your text;  
  2. a bibliography builder which generates a formatted bibliography from those references at the end of your document;  
  3. a configuration function.
